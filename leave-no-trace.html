Leave No Trace

Preserving the beauty and health of the outdoors is a responsibility every camper shares. The Leave No Trace principles guide us to minimize our impact and protect natural environments for future generations.

The 7 Principles of Leave No Trace

  1. Plan Ahead and Prepare: Proper preparation ensures safety and minimizes environmental impacts.
  2. Travel and Camp on Durable Surfaces: Stick to established trails and campsites to avoid damaging vegetation.
  3. Dispose of Waste Properly: Pack out all trash, leftover food, and litter. Leave nothing behind.
  4. Leave What You Find: Preserve the natural environment by not removing rocks, plants, or cultural artifacts.
  5. Minimize Campfire Impact: Use a camp stove for cooking and keep fires small if permitted. Always extinguish fires completely.
  6. Respect Wildlife: Observe animals from a distance and never feed them. Protect their natural behavior.
  7. Be Considerate of Others: Respect other visitors by maintaining a quiet, clean campsite and yielding on trails.

Why It Matters

Practicing Leave No Trace is crucial for protecting the ecosystems we explore. By following these principles, we reduce pollution, prevent wildlife disturbances, and help ensure that the outdoors remains pristine and accessible for everyone.

How You Can Help

  • Educate yourself and others about the Leave No Trace principles.
  • Volunteer for clean-up efforts in your local parks and natural areas.
  • Share your knowledge on social media to inspire responsible outdoor behavior.
  • Lead by example during your own camping trips.
